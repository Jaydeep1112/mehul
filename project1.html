Project Details

Overview

My interest in innovation and problem-solving took on new meaning when I joined the Clever Harvey JuniorMBA in Entrepreneurship — an initiative to replicate actual business situations and build core entrepreneurial abilities. What was initially a formal course soon turned out to be something much more intimate and exhilarating: the creation of my very own clothing brand, Clauset.

Clauset's Birth

For the final project, I envisioned Clauset – Curated Thrift & Designer Fashion — a youth-led, style-focused movement for sustainable fashion. The vision was straightforward: build a vetted platform for second-hand and designer wear that would merge affordability with distinctness. In the era of fast fashion, I wanted to champion the notion that fashion could be distinctive, cyclical, and mindful — simultaneously.

While Clauset was started as a project, it did not remain contained within the classroom. I actually took steps to make it real — from acquiring pieces, establishing a simple inventory system, building a digital presence, and even venturing into brand identity through design and narrative.

Lessons Beyond the Business Plan

Clever Harvey gave me a pragmatic sense of business principles — customer personas, value propositions, unit economics, and marketing — while fostering innovation and risk-taking. But Clauset did something more for me: instilling in me the courage to take an idea and execute it, even if it's not flawless.

This journey provided me with an understanding of branding, sustainability, consumerism, and the harsh realities of starting something from scratch. Although Clauset was not ramped into a proper startup, the experimentation, iteration, and adaptation that took place have proven priceless.

A Spark That Lit a Fire

Clauset is not just a name; it’s a state of mind. One that trusts in making deliberate decisions, owning up to it, and adding value — no matter how little. This project provided me with insight into the life of an entrepreneur and revealed to me that it's not all about profits or products — it's about vision, impact, and purpose.

Now, I bring that entrepreneurial mindset with me into all that I do — whether it's solving problems with innovative thoughts, leading initiatives, or just wondering how things might be done differently.

Objectives

  • Create a youth-led sustainable fashion brand
  • Apply real-world business concepts
  • Learn branding, customer personas & value propositions
  • Build confidence in taking action on ideas
  • Explore fashion's role in conscious consumerism
