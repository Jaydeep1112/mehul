RMUN-2024

RMUN 2024- START OF A LEGACY

Conducting a Model United Nations conference within the school was greater than something that happens it was a test of leadership, teamwork, diplomacy, and strategic thinking. Being one of the main organizing committee members and co-chair of the BLS (Business, Labor & Sustainability) Committee, I was directly responsible for conceptualizing, planning, and implementing a large scale MUN that involved bringing more than 200 student delegates from all grades and schools together.

The Vision: Building a Platform for World Conversations

Our objective was to turn the MUN into something beyond a cold simulation we aspired to make it an alive arena for debate, consciousness, and intellectual interaction. Months of planning went into all the intricacies: creating agendas, allocating committees, inviting off campus chairs, arranging logistics, and having delegates prepped for a worthwhile experience. Our team operated like a actual diplomatic corps bonded by the vision of enabling young people to interact with international challenges.

My Role: Co-Chairing and Leading

I was tasked with ensuring key planning elements operations, timelines, orientation modules for new delegates, and faculty coordination as a committee head. I was also co-chair of the Business, Labor & Sustainability committee, where I led debates on hot global issues like economic inequality, sustainable development, and corporate ethics.

As co-chair, I learned the skill of chairing debate, managing conflicting perspectives, and preserving order and participation all the while promoting critical thinking and equitable diplomacy among the delegates.

I was particularly privileged to be one of the favorite co-chairs among the delegates, recognized for my supportive, engaging, and friendly style of leadership. Most participants valued the open atmosphere I created in which all delegates, regardless of experience, felt heard and respected.

Lessons in Leadership, Diplomacy & Event Management

The MUN taught me much more than what is involved in event planning. I learned:

  • How to work under pressure and deliver to tight deadlines
  • How to solve conflicts and bring a team to stay focused on a shared objective
  • How to both lead with authority and empathy, particularly when mentoring new participants
  • And most critically how to listen, think critically, and work together despite differing perspectives

The experience also taught me how to improve my public speaking, time management, and people skills, and earned me great respect for actual diplomats and negotiators.

Impact and Legacy

The MUN was a tremendous success not just in terms of turnout, but in the depth and maturity of discussions that took place across committees. For many students, it became a stepping stone into public speaking, policymaking, and global affairs. I’m proud to have helped build that platform and even prouder of the confidence, leadership, and sense of purpose it instilled in me.

This experience served to reinforce a basic truth I always have with me: words, when exercised carefully and fearlessly, are capable of moving minds, settling disputes, and bringing about change.

Objectives

  • Host a school wide MUN with 200+ delegates
  • Lead as co-chair of the BLS committee
  • Coordinate schedules, debates, and logistics
  • Promote meaningful global discussion
  • Foster inclusive participation & youth voices

RMUN-2025

RMUN 2025 – Continuing the Legacy

In 2025, I returned to Rustomjee Cambridge International School’s Model United Nations (RMUN), this time as a Chair, carrying forward the legacy that began with our very first RMUN.

It was a dream in 2024, and today it is a tradition of excellence. From 200+ delegates last year to an incredible 350+ delegates this year, RMUN has emerged as one of the most successful student-conferences ever in our school.

My Position as Chair

  • Leadership – Conducted committee sessions in a manner of clarity, inclusiveness, and constructive debate.
  • Mentorship – Assisted first-time delegates with support and encouragement to ensure all voices were represented.
  • Maintaining Legacy – Perpetuated the tradition of diplomacy, respect, and global consciousness that is part of RMUN.
  • Encouraging Development – Witnessed delegates grow from self-doubting speakers to confident negotiators and leaders.

My Development from 2024 to 2025

In 2024, I was a Co-Chair, still learning the ropes; excited but careful, with more preparation than instinct. Coming back in 2025, I noticed a significant improvement. I was much more confident, decisive, and at ease in the chairing role, able to steer the flow of debate, escort delegates with ease, and instill an improved sense of co-operation in the committee.

Highlights of 2025

  • Led a full committee of energetic delegates, leading discussions around world challenges with incisive policy debates and solutions.
  • Presided over an increase in attendance from 200+ to 350+ delegates, helping RMUN reach its all-time high.
  • Implemented more dynamic debate structures to ensure active engagement from first-timers.
  • Earned constructive delegate comments for creating a setting where all voices were heard.
  • Guided several first-time delegates who subsequently indicated interest in taking on leadership roles in subsequent MUNs.

Why 2025 Was Special

RMUN 2025 wasn't merely about resolutions or debates, it was about confidence building, leadership fostering, and a culture of dialogue-enhancing. I came to appreciate as Chair how much I had developed from the past year. It was a circular experience: from learning in 2024 to leading in 2025, from receiving a legacy to actively creating and passing it forward.
