AI for Good: Machine Learning Diagnoses Pneumonia

Project Founder | Speaker | Mentor | Entrepreneur

What began as an autonomous project that united my passion for medicine and technology soon became one of the most significant experiences of my academic career. I created an AI-based diagnostic model and supporting software that can identify pneumonia in chest X-ray images — an endeavor that not only cemented my interest in AI but also allowed me to pay it forward and positively impact my school community and beyond.

The Concept: Applying AI to Fix Actual Problems

Motivated by the vision that technology should be for humanity's benefit, I embarked on discovering how artificial intelligence can be applied in medicine. Through research, trial-and-error coding, and iterating deep learning models, I managed to train a convolutional neural network (CNN) to accurately diagnose pneumonia. Having established this, I integrated the model into an easy-to-use, intuitive software interface — and brought it within the reach of individuals, including those without technical expertise.

This project did not only teach me about machine learning algorithms and medical imaging — but about determination, ethical responsibility, and the power of purpose-driven innovation.

Raising Awareness: Speaking Out for Impact

To make sure my work extended beyond mere technical audiences, I presented at school to illustrate the science of the project and create awareness about the risks of undiagnosed pneumonia. I wanted to show that innovation is not exclusive to labs or corporations — students can do it, too.

This speech created intrigue, particularly with juniors, and resulted in a few questions and discussions regarding AI and health tech. The experience taught me the capability of not just creating solutions — but encouraging others to do so.

‍From Learner to Mentor

A short while later, I started mentoring young students interested in AI and coding. I set up casual weekend sessions where I dissected difficult ideas into bite-sized lessons and encouraged them to work on their own projects. Seeing them tinker, learn, and develop was utterly fulfilling — and it signaled the end of being a lone maker and the beginning of being a community creator.

From Passion to Purpose — and Profession

When news of my work reached them, several students and parents from outside my school community came out wanting to learn about AI. This created a modest but thrilling entrepreneurial prospect — I started providing private workshops and one-on-one guidance, converting my erstwhile hobby into a paying enterprise. The money was secondary to the knowledge that I was assisting in cultivating the next generation of innovators and that they would go on to make a tangible impact.

What This Taught Me

  • Initiative & Innovation: I learned how to take an idea from abstract concept to practical application with real-world effects.
  • Communication: Communicating complex concepts to non-technical people taught me the importance of clarity and empathy.
  • Leadership: Leading others and being accountable for a project taught me responsibility.
  • Entrepreneurship: Translating passion into a sustainable business taught me how to create value — and bring it to market responsibly.

This experience is still one of the most defining chapters of my high school life. It reminded me that technology isn't powerful because it's complicated — it's powerful because of what it can do for people.

AI & Healthcare: Independent Research on Medical Intelligence

Independent Research Project | Bridging AI & Medicine

As an individual who is extremely interested in where technology meets human flourishing, I conducted a personal research project to investigate how Artificial Intelligence patterns can be utilized to transform healthcare — from diagnosis to patient treatment. This was not merely a technical investigation; it was an examination of the ethical, clinical, and technological facets of how machine learning might transform medicine in the coming years.

My work focused on learning and comparing the available AI models applied in the field of healthcare — including medical imaging using convolutional neural networks, natural language processing for healthcare data, and reinforcement learning for optimizing treatment. I read through numerous academic journals, open-source models, and actual use cases from organizations like Stanford and MIT.

Besides theoretical study, I personally tried out a few machine learning libraries (e.g., TensorFlow and PyTorch), modeling diagnostic models and analyzing their performance scores using real-world data samples from NIH and Kaggle.

Key Questions I Explored:

  • How can AI be employed for better early diagnosis and disease detection?
  • What are the ethical considerations of machine-driven medical decisions?
  • Can AI assist in improving healthcare disparities in underprivileged communities?
  • What determines an AI model in healthcare as explainable, reliable, and safe?

Outcomes & Findings

The study led to an in-depth report considering:

  • Strengths and limitations of different types of AI models in healthcare environments
  • How the quality of training data determines model bias
  • Real-world applications across areas such as radiology, oncology, and remote monitoring
  • Challenges in implementing AI ethically in hospitals and clinical processes

Growth Through Inquiry

This study wasn't about coding or data — it was about what responsibility looks like in innovation. It provided me with a first-hand appreciation for how AI needs to be created and implemented with empathy, and always together with human capability.

It taught me to be a scientist — to ask difficult questions, test hypotheses, and remain open to where the data takes me. It also taught me to be a citizen — that solutions for the real world need to be inclusive, ethical, and socially aware.

Sharing & Inspiring

Upon completion of the research, I presented my results to students within my school's computer and science clubs, generating discussion of new areas such as biomedical informatics and AI ethics. I also produced a brief explainer paper for younger children to inform them about the promise and hazards of AI for health.

Why This Matters

This research cemented my passion for working at the intersection of technology and humanity. It reminded me that artificial intelligence is not about machines — it's about improving life, making it safer and fairer, through intelligent, human-focused design.

I now bring this enthusiasm with a dedication to leveraging technology not only for innovation, but for impact.
