Project Details

Overview

Tech Meets Impact: Aiding Faida’s E-Commerce Marketplace

With the mixing of technology and commerce in today's electronic age, the local economies are being transformed in new and groundbreaking ways — and I was fortunate enough to be actively involved in the process. While working as part of a partnership between Span Systems and Faida, an emerging e-commerce platform originating from Africa, I assisted in upgrading and maximizing their platform with the goal of enabling small and medium-sized businesses (SMEs) and local retailers.

My Role: A Bridge Between Tech & Business

I contributed towards enabling technical integration and user experience design of the Faida platform. My roles involved:

  • Helping enhance UI/UX flows to make user navigation easier for buyers and vendors.
  • Being part of backend discussions regarding platform stability, payment gateway integration, and structuring user data.
  • Carrying out user behavior research to assist in making decisions that would enhance marketplace retention and usability.

Closely collaborating with experts at Span Systems, I discovered the ways in which actual e-commerce systems are created, scaled, and sustained — not only from a coding point of view, but also from the business and user-experience aspect.

Why It Mattered

What impressed me most was Faida's mission: democratizing access to digital commerce for underserved communities. Working on this project wasn't merely about developing another tech platform for me — it was about working on a socially oriented digital solution that empowers local vendors to expand their reach, increase their revenue, and join the digital economy.

This experience made me understand the power of transformation that technology holds when leveraged with intention — and the need to link innovation with social good.

Growth & Takeaways

  • Learned to collaborate in a cross-functional environment with both engineers and strategists.
  • Gained exposure to e-commerce architecture, ranging from frontend interfaces to backend APIs.
  • Understood a better appreciation for user-centered design and agile problem-solving.
  • Understood the practical challenges of creating tech for wide-ranging user bases, particularly in developing economies.

What This Experience Means

This was not merely another bullet point on my resume — it was a defining learning experience that brought together my passion for technology, entrepreneurship, and social impact. It provided me a peek into how scalable platforms can be created not for profit, but for purpose.
